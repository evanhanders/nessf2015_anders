\section*{Synergistic activities}
\vspace{-0.25cm}
Brown has been involved in modelling stellar convection since 2003,
when he began using the anelastic spherical harmonic (ASH) code to
study the coupling of convection, rotation and magnetic dynamo action
in the Sun and in other solar-type stars.  He has published results on magnetohydrodynamic
processes in stellar interiors and has extensive experience with fluid
dynamics in stratified atmospheres appropriate to stellar interiors.
He is a member of the closed-source ASH code development team, 
and is a member of the development team for the open-source
Dedalus framework.  Brown has served as a mentor for four
undergraduate students while at University of Wisconsin, Madison,
designing and managing research projects that ranged between 3 months
and 1 year in length, and in one case lead to a succesful undergraduate thesis.
